Functional Piezoelectric Nano Composite Fibers for Smart Drug Delivery

This project proposes the development of mechano-responsive, piezoelectric electrospun nanocomposite fibers for drug delivery applications. By optimizing the structural design and incorporating functional fillers, the piezoelectric efficiency can be enhanced, eliminating the need for additional post-treatment processes and thereby reducing fabrication complexity and cost.

The large surface area inherent to nanomaterials, combined with fiber porosity and transformative piezoelectric properties, enables electrospun fibers to serve as an effective drug-delivery platform driven by the piezoelectric effect.

The on-demand drug release will be tuned using ultrasound stimulation. Overall, this project will demonstrate the utility of piezoelectric nanocomposite electrospun fibers for mechano-responsive, controlled drug release and highlight their potential for biomedical applications.

Supervision: Promotor Prof. Veerle Vandeginste, Prof. Arn Mignon, co-promotor Prof. Hans Hallez, co-promotor Prof. Veerle Bloemen

Postdoc: Fatemeh Mokhtari
