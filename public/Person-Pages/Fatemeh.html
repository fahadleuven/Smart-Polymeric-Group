Fatemeh Mokhtari

Dr. Fatemeh Mokhtari is a research fellow specializing in the design, synthesis, characterization, and modeling of piezoelectric composite materials with applications across multiple sectors.

After completing her PhD in Materials Science at the University of Wollongong (Australia) in 2020, she applied her expertise in polymer modification and materials integration via fiber pultrusion manufacturing while working with KAITEKI (Japan) and the Coal Services Health & Safety Trust (Australia), where she contributed to the development of novel sensor technologies.

In 2022, she continued her career at Deakin University after being awarded the prestigious Alfred Deakin Postdoctoral Research Fellowship, followed by her current role at KU Leuven as a Marie Skłodowska-Curie (MSCA) Postdoctoral Research Fellow in 2024, funded by the European Commission, where she works on piezoelectric nanofibers for smart drug delivery.

Dr. Mokhtari has published 40 papers and book chapters in top-tier journals, two of which were selected as top papers of their respective journals. She has an h-index of 21 and a total of 1,900 citations (GS).
