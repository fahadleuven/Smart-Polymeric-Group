Kenneth Cerdan Gomez

Kenneth Cerdan Gomez was born in Cordoba (Spain). He obtained his BSc in Chemistry and his MSc in Fine Chemistry and Nanochemistry from the University of Cordoba. In 2019, seeking to escape the extremely warm summers of southern Spain, he moved to Belgium to pursue his PhD at KU Leuven under the supervision of Prof. Peter Van Puyvelde. His research, carried out in collaboration with Prof. Guy Van Assche and Dr. Joost Brancart (VUB), focused on the development of novel polymeric materials for applications in self-healing soft robotics. During his PhD, he also led collaborative projects with both academic partners (University of Granada, Spain) and industry (Avantium, The Netherlands).

In 2023, after completing his PhD, he joined the University of California, Santa Barbara, as a postdoctoral researcher co-supervised by Prof. Matthew Helgeson and Prof. Megan Valentine, where he gained extensive expertise in hydrogels and high- throughput experimentation.

Missing the cozy Belgian clouds, he returned to his alma mater, KU Leuven, as a senior postdoctoral researcher in 2025 under the supervision of Prof. Arn Mignon. In collaboration with Prof. Benjamin Gorissen, his current work spans several topics, including the development of stimuli-responsive hydrogels for soft actuators, the electrospinning of polysaccharides for smart wound dressings, and the design of novel ocular adhesives.
