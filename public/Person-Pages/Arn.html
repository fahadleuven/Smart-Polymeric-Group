Arn Mignon

Arn Mignon graduated as Master of Science in Chemical Engineering at Ghent University in 2012. He has worked during his PhD and postdoctoral fellowship on creating stimuli-responsive polymers for various applications. He graduated his PhD in 2016, whereafter he obtained a FWO postdoctoral fellowship. Prior to this, he has worked for 9 months on an INTERREG project. In that period, he has gathered extensive expertise on polymer synthesis, nanoparticle synthesis, processing (electrospinning) and characterization (physico-chemical, thermal, mechanical, visual). Since 2020, he started as Assistant Professor at the faculty of Engineering Technology, focusing his time 50% on educational activities and 50% on research.

The focus of his research lies in the synthesis of new smart polymers and stimuli-responsive polymersomes for biomedical applications (healing of burn wounds and diabetic ulcers, flexor tendon repair, dental implants, support structures for new aortic valves, soft robots to counter inflammatory bowel disease etc.) and the processing of these polymers through different additive manufacturing techniques with a main focus on electrospinning. Since October 2025 he has become Associate Professor, where he will continue to put his focus on these research pillars.
